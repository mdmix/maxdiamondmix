Q and A

Background

Beyond your skillset, do you bring something unique to the table?

Through luck, sound and adventurous choices, I have become a unique mix of personal and professional traits, a unique fun-loving medley of humility, open-mindedness, self-belief, full of motivation to take all kinds of initiatives with people from everywhere.
Having bounced from city-to-city all over, having always been around people with mixed roots, like me, I naturally developed good instincts in interacting with many people, whatever their sensibilities, culture, humor, beyond my video, coding, entrepreneurial, managerial know-how.

In which kind of work-relationship do you perform best?

As much as I relish taking the initiative when I’m committed to my projects, or someone else’s, I also feel very fulfilled co-creating something with stimulating collaborators, as the buzz from that kind of work-relationship can be wonderfully engrossing and inspirational.
In my mind, when everyone is more or less on the same wavelength, humming along in some kind of harmonious cacophony, that type of team-blending can feel very rewarding, like really living-and-breathing work.
So, in short, I’m open to taking on any kind of role at the helm, or with everyone pulling the strings, when we all decide to tack in one specific direction.

What kind of projects appeal to you the most?

I love all challenges, the newer and more multi-faceted they are the better.
Each one, thematically known to me, or brand new, is above all else an opportunity for me to whole-heartedly commit to it.
As my track-record highlights, the purpose of my projects has varied immensely, from the in-depth-classical kind, to the fun, experimental, edgy sort, from colorful bio to gritty blogging sites, from promos to documentaries, and musical montages, all my projects have been full of insights for me, adding much to my current-day profile.

What kind of motivation do you add to the mix?

From the more creative or strategic teamwork, to a vibrant, feel-good workflow, my core purpose is always to make the project-experience highly productive, smooth, and fresh.
And to come up with a clear 'big picture' plan-of-action, to commit wholeheartedly to the most urgent tasks, to be engaged lock, stock, and barrel with all projects, so as to make the most of all of them - and to add to my own ‘in-the-thick-of-things’ enthusiasm.
I love feeling, from the inside, the various forms of the collaborative team spirit in the heat of the deadline-meeting action. And in collaborators, the spreading motivation focus, professional camaraderie, in an atmosphere which is upbeat, encouraging and rewarding, for people who give it their all.
Ultimately, the springboard for all of this is my passionate urge to have a ‘hands-on’ attitude, to make others feel that I am utterly committed, and that a positive, down-to-earth respectful mindset is what I add to the mix.
